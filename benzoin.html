Benzoin Condensation

Definition

Benzoin condensation is a reaction where two molecules of aromatic aldehydes combine in presence of CN⁻ or thiamine to form an α-hydroxy ketone (benzoin).

General Reaction

2 C6H5CHO → C6H5CH(OH)COC6H5

Mechanism (Stepwise)

➡ Step 1: CN⁻ attacks carbonyl carbon

➡ Step 2: Carbanion formation

➡ Step 3: Attack on second molecule (C–C bond)

➡ Step 4: CN⁻ eliminated → Benzoin formed

Key Features

  • Carbon–carbon bond formation
  • Requires nucleophilic catalyst
  • Forms α-hydroxy ketone

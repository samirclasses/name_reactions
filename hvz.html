Hell–Volhard–Zelinsky Reaction

Definition

The Hell–Volhard–Zelinsky (HVZ) reaction is a chemical reaction used to halogenate the alpha (α) carbon of carboxylic acids in the presence of halogen (Cl or Br) and red phosphorus.

General Reaction

R-CH2-COOH + Br2/P → R-CHBr-COOH

Example

CH3COOH + Br2/P → CH2BrCOOH

Mechanism

1. Formation of acyl halide using phosphorus.
2. Enol formation.
3. Halogenation at alpha carbon.
4. Hydrolysis to regenerate carboxylic acid.

Applications

  • Preparation of α-halo acids.
  • Used in synthesis of amino acids.
  • Important intermediate in pharmaceuticals.
  • Helps in organic synthesis pathways.

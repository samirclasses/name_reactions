Grignard's Reagent

Definition

Grignard reagents are organometallic compounds having the general formula R–Mg–X, where R is an alkyl or aryl group and X is a halogen (Cl, Br, I).

They are prepared by reacting an alkyl or aryl halide with magnesium metal in dry ether.

These reagents are highly reactive and act as strong nucleophiles and bases.

Applications

  • Formation of alcohols by reaction with aldehydes and ketones
  • Preparation of carboxylic acids using carbon dioxide
  • Used in synthesis of hydrocarbons
  • Carbon–carbon bond formation (very important in organic synthesis)
  • Preparation of secondary and tertiary alcohols

Limitations

  • Highly sensitive to moisture (reacts with water)
  • Cannot be prepared in presence of alcohol, acids, or water
  • Requires strictly dry conditions (anhydrous ether)
  • Reacts with oxygen and carbon dioxide in air
  • Not compatible with compounds containing acidic hydrogen

Claisen Condensation

Definition

Claisen condensation is a carbon–carbon bond forming reaction in which esters react in the presence of a strong base to form β-keto esters or β-diketones.

General Reaction

R-COOR' → R-CO-CH₂-COOR' (Base)

Example

Ethyl acetate → Ethyl acetoacetate
CH₃COOEt + CH₃COOEt → CH₃COCH₂COOEt

Mechanism (Simplified)

  • Formation of enolate ion
  • Nucleophilic attack on another ester
  • Elimination of alkoxide
  • Formation of β-keto ester

Key Features

  • Occurs in basic medium
  • Requires α-hydrogen
  • Forms C–C bond
  • Produces β-keto esters

Applications

  • Synthesis of β-keto esters
  • Used in pharmaceuticals
  • Carbon chain elongation

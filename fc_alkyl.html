Friedel-Crafts Alkylation

Definition

Friedel-Crafts alkylation is an electrophilic aromatic substitution reaction in which an alkyl group is introduced into an aromatic ring using an alkyl halide in the presence of a Lewis acid catalyst such as AlCl₃.

It was discovered by Charles Friedel and James Crafts in 1877.

General Reaction

Ar–H + R–Cl → Ar–R + HCl (in presence of AlCl₃)

Example

Benzene + CH₃Cl → Toluene (in presence of AlCl₃)

Mechanism

1. Formation of electrophile: Alkyl halide reacts with AlCl₃ to generate a carbocation.

2. Electrophilic attack: The aromatic ring attacks the carbocation forming a sigma complex.

3. Deprotonation: Loss of H⁺ restores aromaticity and forms the alkylated product.

Key Features

  • Occurs via electrophilic substitution
  • Requires Lewis acid catalyst (AlCl₃)
  • Forms C–C bond
  • Carbocation rearrangement possible
  • Polyalkylation may occur

Applications

  • Synthesis of alkylbenzene compounds
  • Used in petrochemical industries
  • Intermediate formation in pharmaceuticals
  • Preparation of dyes and polymers

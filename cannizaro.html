Cannizzaro Reaction

Definition

Cannizzaro reaction is a chemical reaction in which aldehydes having no α-hydrogen undergo self oxidation and reduction (disproportionation) in the presence of a concentrated base such as NaOH or KOH. One molecule of aldehyde is oxidized to a carboxylic acid, while the other is reduced to an alcohol.

General Reaction

2 RCHO + OH⁻ → RCOO⁻ + RCH₂OH

Example

Benzaldehyde undergoes Cannizzaro reaction in presence of concentrated NaOH to give benzyl alcohol and sodium benzoate.

2 C₆H₅CHO → C₆H₅CH₂OH + C₆H₅COONa

Mechanism (Stepwise)

  • Hydroxide ion attacks the carbonyl carbon (nucleophilic addition).
  • Formation of a tetrahedral intermediate.
  • Hydride ion transfer from one aldehyde molecule to another.
  • Formation of alcohol and carboxylate ion.

Key Features

  • Occurs only in aldehydes without α-hydrogen
  • Disproportionation reaction (oxidation + reduction)
  • Requires strong base
  • Produces alcohol and carboxylate salt

Quick Quiz

1. Cannizzaro reaction occurs in:

Aldehydes with α-hydrogen
Aldehydes without α-hydrogen
Ketones

Correct Answer: Aldehydes without α-hydrogen

Applications

  • Preparation of alcohols and acids
  • Industrial synthesis of benzyl alcohol
  • Useful in organic synthesis
